summary: 'I have successfully completed my PhD in Robotics at Carnegie Mellon University, Robotics Institute, advised by Yonatan Bisk. My thesis is titled "Lowering Barriers in Human-Robot Communication."
<br>
<br>
My thesis presents a vision for home robots that understand human intent without explicit commands, built on three contributions: <b>Vid2Robot</b> enables robots to learn tasks from watching human video demonstrations, outperforming prior methods by 20% and showing emergent cross-object motion transfer; <b>ANAVI</b> introduces noise-aware navigation that uses visual features of indoor environments to predict sound propagation and plan quieter paths around people; and <b>ActVQA Arena</b> tackles active visual querying — training robots to manipulate objects and cameras to read barcodes, expiry dates, and labels — revealing that hybrid approaches combining VLM reasoning with RL fine-tuning are needed. Together, these works push toward robots that are both more capable and easier to interact with naturally.
